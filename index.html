Banking In Pennsylvania

The Bank Investigating Committee, appointed by the last Legislature of the State, have made a report, exposing some of the most bold and deliberate frauds that have ever been attempted, under the color of Law. Six banks were organized and four of these were actually put into operation by a set of sharpers, most of them from Buffalo, and some from Ohio, and Indiana, without capital, and these banks had, lately an aggregate circulation of $154,515, with only $9,729 in coin. The banks are managed by a set of unscrupulous, reckless plunderers, and for the circulation the public have no security whatsoever. This is a fair specimen of the banking system adopted by the "anti-bank" politicians, of Pennsylvania, who, while standing on ant-bank platforms, and professing to be opposed to all such institutions, have legislated into life a batch of the meanest, most worthless, and villainoss "wild cat" banks, to be found any place in the United States, outside of Nebraska.

The Democratic Organs Have Done Their Best

They attempted to make party capital out of the affray on Friday night last, in which a man named Tom Allen was killed. To read their remarks, a stranger would be lead to suppose that Allen was a martyr to political persecution, that be was a citizen of spotless fame and immaculate virtue and had died in defending the cause of his country, and his murder has been openly countepanced and excused by the legal authorities of the State and city. All these suppositions are as devoid of truth as the innermost chamber of the Mammoth Cave is of sunlight. There is not the faintest glimmer, or shadow of truth in them, and the organs that are trying to make political capital out of the occurence know it full well.

In this case the statements of the Democratic organs themselves exonerated the officers from any blame whatsoever. The efforts of the Democratoc organs are exceedingly reprehensible. They are productive of no good whatsoever to their party and tend only to excite bad feeling and create serious difficulties.
